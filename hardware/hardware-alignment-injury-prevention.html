Component-by-Component Research Backing

Each component evaluated for its specific role in injury prevention during lifting. Research citations for every claim.

Aligned Camera — MediaPipe Pose Estimation

Layer 1 (free app). Detects visible biomechanical injury markers from a single phone camera.

Strong Knee valgus detection: camera achieves RMSE 3–10° — outperforms body-worn IMUs (RMSE 7–15°) for this ACL risk marker
Mercadal-Baudart et al. (2024), Heliyon; Chia et al. (2021), Med Eng & Physics
Strong Lumbar flexion: RMSE ≤10° for trunk angle and spinal flexion from single phone camera, validated against VICON motion capture during squats, deadlifts, drop jumps
Mercadal-Baudart et al. (2024), Heliyon
Strong Full kinetic chain captured simultaneously — replaces what would require 3–5 body-worn IMU sensors
Direct comparison study: minimum 3 IMUs needed to match single camera view accuracy
Strong PTs cannot visually detect posterior pelvic tilt until it exceeds 34° — camera with angle measurement beats trained human observation
Falk, Aasa & Berglund (2021), Musculoskeletal Science and Practice

Aligned sEMG Sensors — BioAmp EXG Pill / MyoWare 2.0

Layer 2 (premium hardware). Detects what the camera fundamentally cannot see.

Strong Fatigue prediction: EMG median frequency shifts downward 2–5 reps BEFORE visible form breakdown. Accepted biomarker for localized muscle fatigue
Rampichini et al. (2020), Entropy; Cifrek et al. (2009), Clinical Biomechanics
Strong Fatigue classification accuracy: 95.4% using SVM on EMG frequency-domain features
Scientific Reports (2025), hybrid EMG–NMES fatigue study
Strong Compensation detection: malaligned squats show decreased VL/VM/RF activation with increased BF and gastrocnemius compensation — invisible to observation
J Strength Cond Res (2017), muscle activation patterns during squat techniques
Strong H:Q ratio imbalance is a primary contributing factor to ACL injury. Women exhibit reduced ratios indicating quad dominance
Ruas et al. (2019), Sports Medicine - Open; Dedinsky et al. (2017), IJSPT
Strong Kinematics alone underestimates spinal loading — EMG-assisted models are the only way to account for muscle co-contraction forces on the spine
Marras & Granata (1997), J Electromyography & Kinesiology
Gap No published RCT demonstrates sEMG-based feedback directly reduces injury rates during resistance training. Evidence stops at detection accuracy
Battis et al. (2023), Research Square; Seshadri et al. (2022), systematic scoping review

Aligned Vibrotactile Motors — Coin ERM / PM 310-103

Feedback loop — alerts user to detected injury risk signals.

Moderate Vibrotactile localization accuracy: 60.7% on torso — best modality for spatial "where" cueing ("this muscle, not that one")
Deshpande et al. (2021), PMC8541989
Moderate Pre-rep cueing more effective than mid-rep correction for motor learning. Vibration onset 5–100ms is fast enough for between-rep alerts
Partner's research (Muscle Minder finding); Sigrist et al. (2013), Psychonomic Bulletin & Review
Indirect Vibration habituates in 5–15s (Pacinian receptors). Cannot sustain cueing through a rep — only useful for discrete alerts, not continuous guidance
Johansson & Flanagan (2009), Nature Reviews Neuroscience

Partner Only TSA Squeeze Actuators

In partner's spec but not in our current plan. Addresses vibration's limitations.

Moderate Vibration covers 2/6 dimensions of a trainer's hand cue. Pressure covers 4/6: magnitude, duration, release pattern, partial direction
Partner's six-dimension analysis; Deshpande et al. (2021)
Indirect Sustained pressure activates Merkel/Ruffini receptors — these don't habituate. Enables "keep this muscle engaged" cueing through entire rep
Johansson & Flanagan (2009); partner's receptor analysis
Indirect Gradual squeeze release = scaffolding withdrawal. Tests motor independence. Well-established in motor learning theory but not directly studied with TSA for lifting
van Dijk (2006), guidance hypothesis; Lauber & Keller (2014), European J Sport Science
Gap No study has tested TSA squeeze specifically for lifting injury prevention. The mechanism is extrapolated from motor learning + haptic perception research
—
Engineering cost: ~$20–55 per squeeze zone. Adds motors, strings (Dyneema), PTFE routing, calibration algorithm, fail-safe design. Partner's spec covers all of this in detail. The question isn't "does it work?" — it's "is it needed for MVP?"

Excluded IMU Sensors — Both Sides Independently Rejected

Camera replaces everything an IMU does for form tracking, often with better accuracy.

Strong Camera outperforms IMUs for knee valgus: RMSE 3–10° vs 7–15°. IMUs "not recommended for knee frontal-plane kinematic estimations"
Chia et al. (2021), Med Eng & Physics
Strong IMU magnetometers corrupted by ferromagnetic gym equipment (steel plates, racks, cable machines). Gyroscopes drift every ~30 min
Multiple IMU validation studies; partner's spec does not include IMUs
Moderate One thing IMUs do better: bar velocity tracking (ICC 0.91–0.96). But velocity is a performance metric, not an injury prevention metric. Deferrable
Weakley et al. (2021), systematic review of VBT device validity

Where We Diverge — With Evidence

Decision 1 Body Region: Lower Body vs Torso

Our Research: Lower Body + Back

Squats and deadlifts produce the most frequent and most severe beginner injuries — lumbar disc herniation, ACL tears, muscle strains
Bonilla et al. (2022), IJERPH
Erector spinae fatigue during deadlift is the primary mechanism for lumbar injury. EMG detects this before form visibly breaks down
Bakhshinejad et al. (2025), Sports Med - Open
Knee valgus from quad imbalance (VM fatiguing before VL) is the #1 ACL injury mechanism in squats
Ruas et al. (2019); Dedinsky et al. (2017)

Strongest injury prevention case. This is where people actually get hurt.

Partner's Spec: Torso

Torso offers more muscle groups to differentiate between — pecs, lats, traps, delts all distinguishable at 40–60mm two-point discrimination
Partner's skin spatial resolution analysis
Push-ups need zero equipment — best for demo floor. "Your right pec is lazy" narrative is universally relatable
Partner's MVP exercise analysis (Q10)
Shoulder injury during bench press is linked to muscular fatigue + biomechanics — EMG-detectable
Motlagh & Lipps (2024), J Strength Cond Res

Best demo practicality. Easier to show, harder to justify for injury prevention thesis.

Possible bridge: Push-ups are a compound movement activating pecs, delts, AND core (including erector spinae). A torso garment monitoring push-up form validates both the activation coaching narrative AND detects core compensation relevant to injury prevention. Squats added via leg sleeves in v2.

Decision 2 Form Factor: Compression Shirt vs Strap-Harness

Our Research: Compression Shirt

Compression garments produce sEMG signal quality comparable to conventional adhesive electrodes — uniform passive pressure at 15–20 mmHg
Ohiri et al. (2022), Scientific Reports
Compression systems had far fewer experimental failures than strap/electrode attachment systems
PMC 2018, smart compression garment validation
Looks like normal gym wear — critical for beginners who are already intimidated by the gym environment
Product thesis / target user research

Better for the product. Better signal reliability. Less intimidating for target user.

Partner's Spec: Strap-Harness

One adjustable harness fits many body sizes — buckle straps with 15cm adjustment range. No S/M/L/XL sizing problem
Partner's form factor analysis (Q7)
Snap-grid pods are repositionable — iterate on electrode placement without sewing. Electronics removable for washing
Partner's Form Factor B: Modular Vest + Snap-Grid Pods
Leaves skin exposed for camera tracking — compression shirt may occlude body landmarks needed by MediaPipe
Partner's design rationale

Better for R&D. Faster iteration. More flexible. But looks like a research device.

Recommendation: Build both. Strap-harness for R&D — faster iteration, repositionable pods, partner's snap-grid enables rapid A/B testing. Compression shirt for the consumer product — looks normal, passive contact pressure, less intimidating. The harness is the lab tool. The shirt is what ships.
