4-Channel Injury Prevention Prototype

Every component justified by a specific Knowledge Tree finding. 4 sEMG channels covering the two highest-injury body regions (lower back + knees) plus camera for visible form markers and haptics for real-time cueing.

Sensing Layer sEMG

BioAmp EXG Pill × 4
Best-value sEMG AFE · $10/channel · 25.4 × 10mm · <2g each
$40
Channel 1 — Left Erector Spinae
2 electrodes + 1 reference, placed 2–3cm lateral to L3 spinous process on the left. Detects left-side lower back fatigue and co-contraction during deadlift and squat.
Research justification (KT 1.2 + 2.4): Kinematics-only models underestimate spinal loading by 30–40% because they miss muscle co-contraction forces. EMG-assisted models are the only validated approach to estimate true compressive forces on the spine during lifting.
Marras & Granata (1997), J Electromyography & Kinesiology; Granata et al. (2005), Clinical Biomechanics
Channel 2 — Right Erector Spinae
Mirror of Channel 1 on right side. Bilateral pair enables left/right asymmetry detection.
Research justification (KT 2.3): Bilateral asymmetry >10–15% is clinically meaningful and indicates compensation. The left/right erector spinae pair detects when one side of the back is overloading — a precursor to unilateral disc injury.
Ruas et al. (2019), Sports Medicine - Open
Channel 3 — Vastus Lateralis (outer quad)
Electrodes on lateral thigh, 2/3 distance from ASIS to lateral patella border (SENIAM guideline). Dominant or weaker leg.
Channel 4 — Vastus Medialis (inner quad)
Electrodes on medial thigh, ~4cm above medial patella border at 80° angle (SENIAM guideline). Same leg as Channel 3.
Research justification (KT 1.3 + 2.3): When VM fatigues before VL, the knee collapses inward (valgus) — the #1 mechanism for ACL injury during squats. H:Q ratio imbalance is a primary contributing factor to ACL injury. EMG ratio comparison between VL and VM detects this before visible knee deviation.
Ruas et al. (2019); Dedinsky et al. (2017), IJSPT; J Strength Cond Res (2017)
Why BioAmp over MyoWare: At $10/ch vs $43/ch, BioAmp makes 4 channels affordable ($40 vs $172). Pill-size (25.4×10mm, <2g) embeds into compression garment better than MyoWare (52×20mm, 5g). Open-source hardware. Requires solder jumper for EMG mode config and external ADC (ESP32 built-in is adequate for RMS/envelope).

Sourcing: DigiKey (ships from MN, next-day to Austin) or Crowd Supply (ships from Portland, OR). If not in stock: order 2× MyoWare from Amazon as fast-start while BioAmp ships.

Feedback Layer Haptic

10mm Coin Vibration Motors (ERM) × 6
4 active positions + 2 spares · self-adhesive · GPIO + MOSFET drive
$6
Motor 1 — Left erector spinae (co-located with sEMG Ch1)
Vibrates when left erector spinae fatigue threshold crossed. Pre-rep alert: "your left lower back is fatiguing — reduce weight or end set."
Motor 2 — Right erector spinae (co-located with sEMG Ch2)
Vibrates when right side fatigues OR when L/R asymmetry exceeds 15%. Alert: "you're shifting load to one side."
Motor 3 — Vastus medialis (co-located with sEMG Ch4)
Vibrates when VM activation drops relative to VL — the pre-valgus signal. Cue: "activate your inner quad."
Motor 4 — Gluteus medius (lateral hip, no sEMG here)
Vibrates when camera detects knee valgus — directs attention to glute med which stabilizes the knee from above. Cue: "squeeze your glute."
Research justification (KT 4.2 + 4.3): Haptic feedback is most effective for force/effort tasks and shows the least dependency effect of any feedback modality. Pre-rep cueing is justified for safety-critical applications. Vibrotactile localization accuracy is 60.7% on the torso — sufficient for "which body region" cueing. Onset at 5–100ms is fast enough for between-rep alerts.
Sigrist et al. (2013), Psychonomic Bulletin & Review; Deshpande et al. (2021), PMC8541989
Sourcing: Amazon Prime — 10-pack for ~$6 (use 4, keep 6 spares). Drive via 2N7002 N-channel MOSFET ($0.10 each) from ESP32 GPIO.

Vision Layer Camera

Smartphone + MediaPipe Pose Estimation
User's existing phone · No additional hardware cost
$0
What it detects (KT 3.2): Knee valgus angle (RMSE 3–10° — better than IMUs), lumbar flexion / trunk lean (RMSE ≤10°), squat depth, bar path deviation, full-body compensatory movement. 85–95% classification accuracy for correct vs incorrect squat form. Runs at 25–30fps with sub-50ms latency on modern phones.
Mercadal-Baudart et al. (2024), Heliyon; Bhadane et al. (2025), WIREs Data Mining
What it can't detect (KT 3.3 — the gap EMG fills): Muscle fatigue before visible form breakdown. Which muscle is doing the work. Internal spinal loading (co-contraction). Left/right activation asymmetry. The 2–5 rep warning window.
Marras & Granata (1997); Rampichini et al. (2020), Entropy
Setup: Phone propped against wall or water bottle at ~45° angle, 6–8 feet from lifting platform, frontal or sagittal view depending on exercise.

How It All Connects

Sense: 4× BioAmp on erector spinae (L/R) and quads (VL/VM) → analog signals to ESP32 ADC.

Stream: ESP32 computes RMS envelope, streams to phone via BLE at ~4 kB/s.

See: Phone camera runs MediaPipe pose estimation at 30fps — tracks knee angle, trunk lean, squat depth.

Think: Phone app fuses EMG data + camera data. Detects: erector spinae fatigue (RMS rise), quad imbalance (VL/VM ratio diverging), knee valgus (camera), lumbar flexion (camera).

Cue: Phone sends BLE command to ESP32 → fires vibration motor at the relevant muscle location. Pre-rep alert for fatigue, real-time cue for compensation.

Loop time: ~50–200ms from detection to vibration.

What This Prototype Validates

1. Can we get clean sEMG signal from erector spinae and quads during compound lifts?
The #1 risk. If signal quality is too poor through compression fabric during a sweaty deadlift, the entire EMG thesis fails. (KT 6.1)

2. Does the EMG fatigue signal actually precede visible form breakdown?
The core claim: 2–5 rep warning window. If RMS amplitude rise on erector spinae doesn't consistently precede lumbar flexion increase on camera, the EMG layer doesn't add enough value over camera-only. (KT 3.4)

3. Does VL/VM ratio divergence precede visible knee valgus?
Same question for the knee: if quad imbalance on EMG doesn't predict knee valgus on camera, the quad channels aren't earning their place. (KT 1.3 + 2.3)

4. Does haptic cueing change what the beginner does on the next rep?
If vibration at the erector spinae when fatigue is detected doesn't cause the user to reduce weight or improve bracing, the feedback loop isn't closing. (KT 4.2 + 6.3)

5. Will a beginner actually wear this?
If setup takes more than 2 minutes, or the garment looks weird, or the electrodes are uncomfortable, none of the above matters. (KT 6.4)
